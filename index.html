Frequently Asked Questions

Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into your alveolar ridge (jawbone) to serve as artificial tooth roots. Through a process called osseointegration, the implant fuses with your bone tissue, creating a stable foundation for fixed or removable dental prostheses. This implant biocompatibility ensures long-term stability and function. Once healed, a custom crown, bridge, or denture is attached to complete your smile restoration. The result is a natural-looking and functioning tooth replacement that can last a lifetime with proper care.

The implant surgical procedure typically takes 1-2 hours per implant, depending on complexity. Our implant oral surgeon uses advanced techniques to ensure precision and comfort. After placement, the healing period for implant integration usually ranges from 3-6 months, during which the implant bonds with your bone. Most patients experience minimal discomfort and can return to normal activities within a few days. We provide detailed post-operative instructions and are available for any concerns during your recovery. Temporary restorations can often be placed so you're never without teeth during the healing process.

Most adults with missing teeth are excellent candidates for dental implants. The key requirements are adequate bone density in the alveolar ridge, healthy gum tissue, and overall good health. Our implant periodontist will perform a comprehensive evaluation including digital imaging to assess your bone structure and create a personalized implant planning strategy. Even if you have experienced bone loss, we offer solutions such as bone grafting to prepare your jaw for successful implant placement.
